Frequently Asked Questions

What is EXIF data?

EXIF is metadata embedded in photos by cameras and phones — including GPS coordinates, device model, date taken, and camera settings.

How does this tool remove metadata?

We redraw the image pixels onto a new canvas and export a fresh file. Only pixel data is kept — all embedded metadata is discarded.

Is the image uploaded?

No. Metadata removal happens entirely in your browser. Your photo never leaves your device.
